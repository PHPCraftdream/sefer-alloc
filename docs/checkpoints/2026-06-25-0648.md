## Decisions
- **Membrane Inversion** over polishing the byte tier: the safe slot-table
  discipline governs OS segments (metadata self-hosts in managed segments)
  instead of consuming `Vec<T>` ‚ÄĒ the one change that makes a sound global
  allocator possible. Rejected: extending `ByteRegion`/`ShardedByteArena` (they
  call std-collections/`std::alloc` inside alloc ‚Üí reentrancy; superseded, not
  extended).
- **Two faces, one substrate**: `Handle` and `*mut u8` are views of the same
  `(segment,offset)` memory ‚ÄĒ do NOT fork the project into "handle store" vs
  "malloc". Reconciles the earlier Path-A-vs-Path-B fork into one architecture.
- **Direct OS aperture replaces `std::alloc`** (mmap/VirtualAlloc) ‚ÄĒ the
  `std::alloc` fallback recurses when we are the global allocator.
- **Single-writer lock-free hot path** (per-thread heap + intrusive free lists),
  Mutex only off the hot path; reuse Phase-7 organs (Region‚Üímetadata,
  AtomicSlot/epoch‚Üícross-thread free, ShardedRegion‚Üíper-thread heap, 7b‚Üíthread-free
  list, 7c‚Üíheap==core) ‚ÄĒ Phase 8+ is a re-basing refactor, not green-field.
- **prewarm fixes cold-start latency, NOT benchmark visibility** ‚ÄĒ added it but
  documented honestly that the microbench is spawn-dominated; seeing the sharding
  win needs a persistent-pool bench (Phase 5/11 territory).
