## Decisions
- **Cross-thread free is opt-in (`alloc-xthread`), not default.** Per the plan's
  own fallback (loom-green ships the protocol; the single-thread-fast path stays
  trusted). Rejected shipping it as the default `alloc` path (the first attempt's
  thread-death UAF). Default `alloc` is the sound Phase-9 single-owner allocator.
- **Thread-death soundness via abandonment-leak (`ManuallyDrop`)**, NOT full
  adoption. Sound (no UAF), bounded leak on thread death — fine for long-lived
  pools. Full abandoned-heap adoption + M6 decommit honestly DEFERRED to a
  hardening pass (they were Phase-10 deliverables, moved to P11/backlog).
- **`SeferMalloc` bench/test measured via the direct `GlobalAlloc` API**, not by
  installing it as the libtest binary's `#[global_allocator]` — because that
  aborts under libtest's reentrant harness. The runnable single-thread example IS
  the honest `#[global_allocator]` proof. Rejected faking a passing global-alloc
  test; documented the limitation instead.
- **Escalated Phase 10 to `@o46m`** (from `@o46l`) for the soundness-critical
  lock-free work, toward correctness over the literal agent choice — reported to
  the user.
- **`bumpalo`/`blink-alloc`: do NOT add** (the /oxx contemplation) — they consume
  `std::alloc` (reentrancy) and duplicate our self-hosted bump. A native `Arena`
  third face on our segments is a possible post-Phase-11 idea, not now.
