## Decisions
- **Phase 12 keystone — one inversion, three cures:** heaps → global self-hosted
  registry (slot table carved from segments); TLS → raw `Cell<*mut HeapCore>`
  cache (no `RefCell`); thread-exit → abandon to registry (not RAII drop). Solves
  reentrancy-safe TLS + abandoned-heap adoption + M6 decommit together. Rejected:
  patching the three independently.
- **Segment-centric free state (Phase 12 refactor):** move small free-lists from
  `Heap.bins` into the per-segment `BinTable` (already built in Phase 8, bypassed
  since Phase 9) so segments are self-describing and adoption is O(1) (no bin
  merging). Supersedes Phase-9 heap-local bins.
- **Never return null from the malloc face (M10):** a process-global fallback
  heap serves the pre-/post-TLS windows; null→process-abort is designed out.
- **bumpalo/blink-alloc: not added** — they consume `std::alloc` (reentrancy) and
  duplicate our self-hosted bump; a native `Arena` third face is the faithful
  path (post-Phase-13, not scheduled).
- **Phase 13 speed plan** — highest leverage: O(1) `const SIZE2CLASS[]` lookup
  (replace the per-alloc linear scan) + raw-pointer TLS (from P12) + inlined
  arithmetic own-thread free (no magic read on hot path); then two-list free,
  refill tuning, heap==core pinning, macro-bench (mstress/larson).
